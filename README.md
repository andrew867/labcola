## üìö Scientific Background

### Mass Spectrometry Analysis
The project utilized two types of mass spectrometers to analyze:
- Authentic Coca-Cola
- Individual essential oils
- Competitor sodas (Pepsi, etc.)
- Progressive recipe iterations

### Key Chemical Compounds Identified
- **Œ±-Terpineol**: Dominant peak, forms from citrus oil breakdown
- **Limonene**: Primary citrus character
- **Cinnamaldehyde**: Cinnamon spice notes
- **4-Terpineol**: Fresh, green notes from tea tree oil
- **Furfural**: From caramel color, adds depth
- **S-Linalool**: Proves coriander is included (not lavender/neroli)

### Chemical Reactions
1. **Sucrose Hydrolysis**: 104g sucrose ‚Üí ~116g glucose/fructose
2. **Terpene Transformation**: Limonene ‚Üí Œ±-terpineol (acid-catalyzed)
3. **Ester Formation**: Fructose + heat + acid ‚Üí ethyl levulinate

## üéØ Quick Start for Making Lab-Cola

Want to jump right in? Here's what you need:

### Essential Equipment
- Adjustable micropipette (up to 1000ŒºL)
- Precision scale (0.01g accuracy)
- Glass containers (no metal!)
- 50mL graduated cylinder
- Heat-resistant glassware
- SodaStream or carbonated water source

### Essential Ingredients
- Essential oils (lemon, lime, orange, tea tree, cinnamon, nutmeg, coriander)

- Caffeine powder
- Glycerin
- 85% phosphoric acid
- Wine tannins
- Caramel color (Shank's brand)
- Cane sugar
- 5% vinegar
- Vanilla extract
